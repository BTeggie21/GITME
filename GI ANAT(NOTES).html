🗺️ MASTER FRAMEWORK: THE "5-LAYER GI MAP"

For EVERY abdominal organ, ask these 5 questions in order:

Layer Question Why It Matters Example: Gallbladder
1️⃣ LOCATION Where is it? (quadrant/region, peritoneal status) Guides physical exam, imaging interpretation, surgical approach RUQ, visceral surface of liver, between segments IV & V
2️⃣ SUPPLY Artery? Vein? Lymph? Predicts ischemia patterns, metastasis routes, portal HTN effects Cystic artery (right hepatic), portal drainage, cystic nodes (Calot's triangle)
3️⃣ NERVE Parasympathetic? Sympathetic? Sensory? Explains pain referral, motility disorders, surgical complications Vagus (stimulates), T7-T9 sympathetic (inhibits), referred to RUQ/right scapula
4️⃣ FUNCTION What does it DO? Links anatomy to physiology → clinical reasoning Stores & concentrates bile, releases with CCK/fatty meals
5️⃣ CLINICAL What breaks here? Anchors facts to real cases → long-term retention Cholecystitis, cholecystectomy, Calot's triangle danger
💡 Study hack: When reviewing any organ, verbally walk through all 5 layers. If you skip one, your brain flags it as "incomplete."

🧠 INTEGRATED ORGAN CHEAT SHEETS (5-LAYER FORMAT)

🫀 LIVER + GALLBLADDER (FOREGUT PAIR)

1️⃣ LOCATION
  • Liver: RUQ, under ribs 7-11, diaphragmatic + visceral surfaces
  • Bare area: No peritoneum → contacts diaphragm → drains to mediastinal nodes
  • Gallbladder: Fossa between segments IV/V, visceral surface of liver
  • Peritoneal status: Liver = intraperitoneal (except bare area); Gallbladder = intraperitoneal
2️⃣ SUPPLY
  • Artery:
    • Liver: Proper hepatic (celiac → common hepatic) → right/left hepatic
    • Gallbladder: Cystic artery (usually from right hepatic) ⚠️ 25-50% variations!
  • Vein:
    • Liver: Portal vein (70%, nutrient-rich) + Hepatic artery (30%, O₂-rich)
    • Drainage: Hepatic veins → IVC
    • Gallbladder: Small veins → hepatic parenchyma → portal system
  • Lymph:
    • Liver: Deep → hepatic nodes → celiac; Superficial → porta hepatis/parasternal
    • Gallbladder: Cystic nodes (Calot's) → porta hepatis → celiac
3️⃣ NERVE
  • Parasympathetic: Vagus (CN X) → stimulates bile production, gallbladder contraction
  • Sympathetic: T7-T9 via celiac plexus → inhibits gallbladder, vasoconstriction
  • Sensory:
    • Liver capsule: Lower intercostals (T7-T11) → sharp, localized RUQ pain
    • Gallbladder: Sympathetic afferents T7-T9 → referred to RUQ/epigastrium/right scapula
4️⃣ FUNCTION
  • Liver: Bile production, detox, glycogen storage, protein synthesis, vitamin activation
  • Gallbladder: Concentrates/stores bile → releases with CCK/fatty meals
5️⃣ CLINICAL
  • Cholecystitis: Cystic duct obstruction → RUQ pain (visceral → parietal), Murphy's sign +
  • Cholecystectomy danger: Calot's triangle (cystic artery, cystic duct, common hepatic duct)
  • Liver trauma: Bare area injury → retroperitoneal bleed; Capsule stretch → sharp pain
  • Portal HTN: Esophageal varices (left gastric ↔ azygos), caput medusae, hemorrhoids
🎯 Active Recall:
Q: "During cholecystectomy, you see an artery running along the cystic duct. What is it, and where does it usually come from?"
A: Cystic artery → usually from right hepatic artery (but variations common!)

🦴 POSTERIOR ABDOMINAL WALL (THE "BACKDOOR" TO ABDOMEN)

1️⃣ LOCATION
  • Boundaries: T12-L5 vertebrae, 12th rib, iliac crest, psoas/QL muscles
  • Key space: Retroperitoneum (behind parietal peritoneum)
2️⃣ SUPPLY
  • Artery: Abdominal aorta (T12-L4) → lumbar, renal, gonadal, IMA branches
  • Vein: IVC (right of aorta) ← lumbar, renal, gonadal veins
  • Lymph: Para-aortic (lumbar) nodes ← kidneys, gonads, uterus, adrenals
3️⃣ NERVE

Lumbar plexus (L1-L4) EMBEDDED in psoas major:

  • Iliohypogastric (L1): Suprapubic sensation
  • Ilioinguinal (L1): Scrotum/labia, medial thigh
  • Genitofemoral (L1-L2): Cremasteric reflex
  • Lateral femoral cutaneous (L2-L3): Lateral thigh (meralgia paresthetica!)
  • Femoral (L2-L4): Anterior thigh motor/sensory
  • Obturator (L2-L4): Medial thigh adductors
4️⃣ FUNCTION
  • Structural support for abdominal organs
  • Conduit for vessels/nerves to lower limb + pelvis
  • Retroperitoneal space = "highway" for surgical access (kidney, aorta)
5️⃣ CLINICAL
  • Psoas sign: Pain on hip extension → retrocecal appendicitis or psoas abscess
  • AAA: Pulsatile mass at L4 (aortic bifurcation) + back pain = surgical emergency
  • Meralgia paresthetica: Tight belt compresses lateral femoral cutaneous nerve → lateral thigh numbness
  • Lumbar puncture: Safe at L3-L4 or L4-L5 (below spinal cord end at L1-L2)
🎯 Active Recall:
Q: "A construction worker has burning lateral thigh pain. He wears a tight tool belt. What nerve, and why no motor deficit?"
A: Lateral femoral cutaneous nerve (L2-L3) → purely sensory → compression at inguinal ligament

🧭 CLINICAL DECISION TREE: "WHERE IS THE PAIN?"

Patient presents with abdominal pain → Follow this algorithm:

Step Action Key Questions/Findings
STEP 1 LOCALIZE THE PAIN
  • Epigastric → FOREGUT (stomach, gallbladder, pancreas, duodenum 1st-2nd)
  • Periumbilical → MIDGUT (appendix, small bowel, cecum)
  • Hypogastric → HINDGUT (sigmoid, rectum)
  • Localized + sharp + worse with movement → PARIETAL PERITONEUM (late appendicitis, perforation)
STEP 2 ADD ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS
  • RUQ pain + fever + Murphy's sign + → Cholecystitis
  • Epigastric pain relieved by food → Duodenal ulcer
  • Epigastric pain worsened by food → Gastric ulcer
  • Periumbilical pain migrating to RLQ → Appendicitis
  • LLQ pain + fever + change in bowel habits → Diverticulitis
  • Flank pain + hematuria → Ureteric stone (retroperitoneal)
STEP 3 CHECK FOR RED FLAGS
  • Hematemesis + cirrhosis → Esophageal varices (portal HTN)
  • Pulsatile abdominal mass + back pain → AAA (emergency!)
  • Jaundice + painless → Pancreatic head CA (obstructive)
  • Weight loss + change in bowel habits + anemia → Colorectal CA
STEP 4 RECALL THE ANATOMY
  • "If it's foregut → celiac artery → vagus → T5-T9 → epigastric → portal drainage → celiac nodes"
  • "If it's midgut → SMA → vagus → T9-T12 → umbilical → SMV → superior mesenteric nodes"
  • "If it's hindgut → IMA → pelvic splanchnic → L1-L2 → hypogastric → IMV → inferior mesenteric nodes"
