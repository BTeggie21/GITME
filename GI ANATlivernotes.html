📍 Surfaces & Landmarks

Diaphragmatic (Antero-superior) Surface
• Lies below diaphragm; separated by peritoneum
• Cardiac impression centrally (heart position)
• Majority under right dome of diaphragm
Visceral (Postero-inferior) Surface
• Bounded by inferior liver edge
• Contains: porta hepatis, gallbladder fossa, fissures for ligamentum teres/venosum
• Quadrate lobe (anterior to porta hepatis)
• Caudate lobe (posterior to porta hepatis)

⚠️ Bare Area

Key Facts:
• Direct contact with diaphragm; NO peritoneum
• Bounded by coronary ligaments
• IVC groove at medial end
• Clinical: Allows direct spread of infection between liver & diaphragm

⚙️ Functional Anatomy (Couinaud System)

Core Concept: Segments based on portal vein distribution (not external landmarks). Each segment has independent vascular inflow, outflow, and biliary drainage.

📐 Key Fissures

Cantlie's Line (Main Portal Fissure)
• Extends: midpoint of gallbladder fossa → midpoint of IVC
• Contains: Middle hepatic vein
• Separates: Functional right vs left hemi-liver
• Discriminator: Surgical resection follows this line, NOT falciform ligament
Other Critical Fissures
Left Portal Fissure: Contains left hepatic vein; divides left hemi-liver
Right Portal Fissure: Most variable; traverses thickest parenchyma (surgical risk)
Umbilical Fissure: Contains ligamentum teres + left portal vein branch
Venous Fissure: Contains ligamentum venosum (obliterated ductus venosus)
Rouvière's Sulcus: Behind gallbladder fossa; landmark for right posterior pedicle

Duct (CBD) - Artery (Hepatic) - Vein (Portal)
Arranged: Duct (anterior-right), Artery (anterior-left), Vein (posterior)

Support Hierarchy

Primary: IVC + Hepatic veins (especially right) — "suspend" liver
Secondary: Right kidney, hepatic flexure, pancreas/duodenum
Tertiary: Falciform ligament (minor role)

🎯 High-Yield Function Facts

Bile Production:
• Liver makes bile → stored/concentrated in gallbladder
• Bile salts: Emulsify fats (digestive role)
• Bilirubin: Excretory pigment from RBC breakdown
• Released into duodenum in response to CCK
Storage Functions:
• Fat-soluble vitamins: A, D, E, K
• Water-soluble: B₁₂
• Minerals: Iron (ferritin), Copper
• Energy: Glycogen (up to 10% liver weight)

🔄 Biliary Tree Flow

Intrahepatic ducts → Right/Left hepatic ducts → Common hepatic duct + Cystic duct → Common bile duct → Ampulla of Vater → Duodenum

⚠️ Critical Variations & Pitfalls

Luschka's Duct (Subvesical Duct)
• Small duct from segment V traversing gallbladder fossa
• Clinical: If missed during cholecystectomy → bile leak → biloma
• Must be identified and ligated
Cystic Duct Anatomy
• Length: 2-4 cm; Diameter: 2-3 mm
• Spiral mucosal folds → maintain patency (NOT flow regulation)
• Joins common hepatic duct at variable level (usually middle third)
• Discriminator: Right hepatic duct = short/vertical; Left = longer/horizontal

🔺 Surgical Triangles

Calot's Triangle:
• Boundaries: Common hepatic duct (base), Cystic duct & artery (sides)
• Contains: Cystic artery (critical to identify in cholecystectomy)
• Clinical: Misidentification → iatrogenic bile duct injury

🏥 Clinical Correlates

🩺 High-Yield Clinical Scenarios
Cholecystectomy Effects:
• Bile flows continuously (not stored) → less concentrated
• Early post-op: steatorrhea (fatty stools) due to impaired fat digestion
• Usually resolves as bowel adapts
Mirizzi Syndrome:
• Gallstone impacted in cystic duct/Hartmann's pouch
• Extrinsic compression of common hepatic duct → obstructive jaundice
• Risk of bile duct injury during surgery
Courvoisier's Law:
• Painless jaundice + palpable gallbladder = malignant obstruction (e.g., pancreatic CA)
• NOT typical of gallstone disease (gallbladder usually fibrotic/non-distensible)
Porcelain Gallbladder:
• Calcification of gallbladder wall
• ↑ Risk of gallbladder carcinoma → prophylactic cholecystectomy often recommended
Bile Duct Injury (Iatrogenic):
• Most common during laparoscopic cholecystectomy
• Mechanism: Misidentification of CBD as cystic duct
• Prevention: Critical view of safety in Calot's triangle

❓ Self-Test Questions

Click "Show Answer" to check. Great for active recall!

1. Cantlie's line connects which two landmarks?
✅ Midpoint of gallbladder fossa to midpoint of IVC. Contains middle hepatic vein; separates functional right/left liver.
2. Which Couinaud segment corresponds to the caudate lobe?
✅ Segment I. Unique: drains into both right and left hepatic ducts; has independent blood supply.
3. A patient has painless jaundice and a palpable, non-tender gallbladder. What does Courvoisier's law suggest?
✅ Malignant obstruction. Gallstones typically cause fibrotic, non-distensible gallbladder; malignancy allows dilation.
4. During cholecystectomy, failure to ligate which structure can cause post-op bile leak?
✅ Luschka's duct. Small accessory duct from segment V traversing gallbladder fossa; if missed → bile leak → biloma.
5. What is the primary functional basis for Couinaud's liver segmentation?
✅ Distribution of portal venous branches. Each segment has independent portal inflow, hepatic venous outflow, and biliary drainage.
