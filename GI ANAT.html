🎯 HIGH-YIELD MEMORY AIDS

Arterial Mnemonics:

  • Celiac trunk branches: "Left Gastric, Splenic, Common Hepatic" → Liver, Stomach, Heart
    Mnemonic: Let's Share Health
  • SMA branches: "I See 3 Right Colics"
    → Inferior pancreaticoduodenal, Jejunal, Ileal, Ileocolic, Right colic, Middle colic
  • IMA branches: "Left Colic, Sigmoid, Superior Rectal" → Left Side Rectum

Portosystemic Anastomoses (4 Sites):

ERUR: Esophagus, Rectum, Umbilicus, Retroperitoneum
"When portal pressure's high, these veins will lie!"
Site Portal Vein Connection Systemic Vein Connection Clinical Result
Esophagus Left gastric vein Azygos vein → SVC Esophageal varices
Rectum Superior rectal vein Middle/inferior rectal → internal iliac Hemorrhoids
Umbilicus Paraumbilical veins Superficial epigastric → femoral Caput medusae
Retroperitoneum Veins of Retzius Lumbar/renal veins Retroperitoneal varices

Lymph Node Metastasis Patterns:

  • Gastric CA → Virchow's node (left supraclavicular/Troisier's sign)
  • Testicular CA → Para-aortic nodes (NOT inguinal; embryologic origin at L2)
  • Rectal CA:
    • Upper (above pectinate) → Inferior mesenteric/para-aortic
    • Lower (below pectinate) → Internal iliac/inguinal nodes
  • Pancreatic CA → Celiac/SMA nodes → Virchow's node

Nerve Injury Correlations:

  • Vagus damage: ↓ gastric motility, ↓ acid secretion, gastroparesis, delayed gastric emptying
  • Pelvic splanchnic (S2-S4) damage: ↓ rectal motility, urinary retention, sexual dysfunction (erection/ejaculation)
  • Lower intercostal nerve damage: Loss of abdominal wall sensation, impaired cough, weakened abdominal reflexes
  • Ilioinguinal/iliohypogastric injury: Post-herniorrhaphy groin pain, sensory loss over suprapubic region
📌 Study Tip: Focus on patterns (e.g., all foregut organs share celiac artery + vagus + T5-T9 sympathetic) rather than isolated facts.
